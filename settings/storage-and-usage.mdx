Every ORArchive workspace has a storage quota that applies to all files, records, and uploaded content within it. Keeping an eye on your usage helps you avoid unexpected interruptions to uploads and ensures your team's archive remains fully accessible. You can view a detailed breakdown of your consumption, take immediate action to reclaim space, or upgrade your plan — all from the Storage settings page.

---

## Viewing Your Storage Usage

The Storage settings page gives you a live summary of how much space your workspace is consuming and how it's distributed across different content types.

To access it, navigate to **Settings > Storage**. At the top of the page, a **progress bar** displays your total used storage against your plan's total available quota (for example, *34.2 GB used of 100 GB*). Below the progress bar, a breakdown by file type shows exactly where your storage is going:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Documents" icon="file-lines" href="/records/uploading-files">
    PDFs, Word documents, spreadsheets, presentations, and other office file formats.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Images" icon="image" href="/records/uploading-files">
    JPEG, PNG, TIFF, GIF, SVG, and other image formats attached to records.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Video" icon="video" href="/records/uploading-files">
    MP4, MOV, AVI, and other video files uploaded as record attachments.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Audio" icon="volume-high" href="/records/uploading-files">
    MP3, WAV, AAC, and other audio formats stored within the workspace.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Any content that doesn't fall into the above categories — such as ZIP archives, binary files, or custom format exports — is counted under **Other**.

## Storage Limits by Plan

ORArchive offers four plans with different storage capacities, file size limits, and team size allowances. Review the table below to understand what your current plan includes.

| Plan | Storage | Max File Size | Team Members |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | 10 GB | 512 MB | 3 |
| **Starter** | 100 GB | 2 GB | 10 |
| **Professional** | 1 TB | 5 GB | Unlimited |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Custom | Unlimited |

<Accordion title="What counts toward my storage quota?">
  All files attached to records, record version history snapshots, files in your Trash (not yet permanently deleted), and workspace logos or assets count toward your total storage quota. Shared records contributed by external collaborators who are not workspace members count toward the workspace that owns the record, not the collaborator's account.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if I'm on the Free plan and exceed 10 GB?">
  If your workspace reaches its storage limit, new uploads are paused. Existing records remain fully accessible and searchable — you just won't be able to add new files until you either free up space or upgrade your plan.
</Accordion>

## Freeing Up Storage

If your workspace is approaching its limit, several actions can help you reclaim space quickly without requiring a plan upgrade.

**1. Empty the Trash**

Files you delete in ORArchive move to the Trash rather than being permanently removed. Records sitting in Trash still count toward your storage quota. To permanently remove them, go to **Settings > Storage > Empty Trash** and confirm the action. This is often the fastest way to recover a significant amount of space.

**2. Delete large files you no longer need**

Review and remove files that are no longer relevant to active workflows — such as superseded exports, duplicate uploads, or raw media files that have already been processed elsewhere.

**3. Remove old record versions**

ORArchive preserves the full edit history of every record by default. If version history has accumulated significantly, you can delete older snapshots you no longer need by opening the record, navigating to the **History** tab, and clicking the delete icon next to any version you want to remove. The current version is always preserved.

<Tip>
  Use the **Filter by File Size** option in the main records view to sort your entire archive from largest to smallest. This surfaces your biggest files immediately, so you can make targeted decisions about what to remove rather than hunting through folders manually.
</Tip>

## Upgrading Your Plan

When your team's storage needs outgrow your current plan, upgrading takes only a few minutes and storage becomes available immediately after payment is confirmed.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Billing Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings > Billing** using the avatar menu in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Upgrade Plan">
    Click the **Upgrade Plan** button. A plan comparison modal will appear showing the storage, file size, and team member limits for each available tier.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your plan">
    Choose the plan that meets your needs — **Starter**, **Professional**, or **Enterprise** — and review the pricing details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete payment">
    Enter your billing details and confirm the purchase. For Enterprise plans, you'll be directed to contact the sales team to configure a custom quote.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start using your new storage">
    Your upgraded storage quota is available immediately after payment is processed. No restart or migration is required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Billing in ORArchive is **per workspace**, not per individual user. If you are a member of multiple workspaces, each workspace maintains its own independent subscription and storage quota. Upgrading one workspace does not affect the others.
</Note>

<Warning>
  If your workspace exceeds its storage limit, all new uploads are paused for every member of that workspace until you either upgrade to a higher plan or free up enough space to fall below the quota. Records already stored remain fully accessible during this time — only new uploads are blocked.
</Warning>
