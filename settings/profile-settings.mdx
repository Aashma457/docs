Your ORArchive account settings give you full control over how your profile appears to collaborators, how you authenticate, and — if you're an Admin or Owner — how your shared workspace is configured. Keep your details accurate and your security settings up to date to get the most out of the platform.

## Your Profile

Your profile stores the personal details that identify you across ORArchive — in record activity feeds, shared collections, and comment threads. You can access your profile settings by clicking your **avatar** in the top-right corner of any page and selecting **Settings**, or by navigating directly to [app.orachive.com/settings/profile](https://app.orachive.com/settings/profile).

The following fields are available on your profile page:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Display Name** | The name visible to collaborators across the workspace |
| **Email Address** | Your login email and the address used for notifications |
| **Job Title** | Optional role label shown on your profile card |
| **Profile Photo** | Upload a JPEG or PNG (max 5 MB) to personalize your avatar |
| **Language** | Sets the interface language for your account |
| **Time Zone** | Controls how timestamps and scheduled notifications are displayed |

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Profile Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings > Profile** using the avatar menu in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update your fields">
    Edit any of the available fields — Display Name, Email Address, Job Title, Profile Photo, Language, or Time Zone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save Changes** at the bottom of the form. A confirmation banner will appear once your profile has been updated successfully.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Set your **Time Zone** to your local region to ensure that activity timestamps, record history entries, and scheduled notification digests all display at the correct local time. This is especially important if you collaborate with teammates across multiple time zones.
</Tip>

## Changing Your Password

You can update your password at any time from the Security section of your settings. Choose a strong, unique password that you don't reuse across other services.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Security Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings > Security**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initiate a password change">
    Click **Change Password** to expand the password update form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your passwords">
    Type your **current password** in the first field, then enter your **new password** and confirm it in the fields below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the update">
    Click **Update Password**. You will remain signed in on your current device, but other active sessions may be prompted to re-authenticate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your new password must meet the following requirements: **at least 8 characters**, **one uppercase letter**, and **one number**. Passwords that do not meet these criteria will be rejected before submission.
</Note>

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds a second layer of security to your account by requiring a time-based one-time code in addition to your password when you sign in. ORArchive supports any TOTP-compatible authenticator app, including Google Authenticator, Authy, and 1Password.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open 2FA Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings > Security > Two-Factor Authentication**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable 2FA">
    Click **Enable 2FA**. A setup dialog will appear with a QR code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open your authenticator app and scan the QR code displayed on screen. If you can't scan the code, use the manual entry key shown below the QR code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the setup">
    Enter the **6-digit code** generated by your authenticator app into the verification field, then click **Verify**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your backup codes">
    ORArchive will display a set of single-use backup codes. Download or copy these codes and store them in a secure location — such as a password manager or encrypted file.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If you lose access to your authenticator app and do not have your backup codes, you will be locked out of your account. ORArchive support can assist with account recovery, but this process requires identity verification and may take time. **Store your backup codes securely before completing setup.**
</Warning>

## Workspace Settings

Workspace settings control the shared environment that all members of your team operate within. These settings affect record defaults, branding, and organizational structure across the entire workspace.

<Note>
  Only users with the **Owner** or **Admin** role can view and modify workspace settings. If you do not see this section in your Settings menu, contact your workspace Owner to request elevated permissions.
</Note>

The following workspace-level options are available under **Settings > Workspace**:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Workspace Name" icon="pen" href="/settings/profile-settings">
    The name displayed in the workspace switcher and on shared links. Choose something recognizable to your team and external collaborators.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Workspace Logo" icon="image" href="/settings/profile-settings">
    Upload a logo (JPEG or PNG, max 2 MB) that appears in the top-left navigation bar and on branded share pages.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Default Category" icon="folder" href="/organizing/folders-and-categories">
    Set the category automatically applied to newly created records, saving your team time during uploads.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Default Record Visibility" icon="eye" href="/sharing/user-roles-and-permissions">
    Choose whether new records default to **Private** (visible only to you) or **Team** (visible to all workspace members). Individual records can always be adjusted after creation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
