ORArchive indexes every record's title, description, tags, custom metadata, and text note content so that finding a specific document never requires you to remember exactly where you filed it. Whether you know a keyword, a date range, or just the person who uploaded a file, the search and filter tools let you narrow thousands of records down to the exact set you need.

## Basic Search

Press **/** from anywhere in the app, or click the search bar at the top of the main content area, to open the global search. Start typing any keyword — results appear in real time as you type, ranked by relevance across the following content:

- Record titles
- Record descriptions
- Tags
- Text note content attached to records
- Custom metadata field values

Results from all folders and collections you have access to are included in one unified list. Click any result to jump directly to that record.

<Tip>
  Wrap your query in **double quotes** to search for an exact phrase rather than individual keywords. For example, searching `"annual report"` returns only records that contain that precise sequence of words, filtering out records that contain "annual" and "report" separately. Exact-phrase search is especially useful for locating records with specific reference numbers or legal terminology.
</Tip>

## Filter Panel

For more targeted retrieval, use the filter panel to layer conditions on top of your search results — or browse without any keyword at all. Click the **funnel icon** to the right of the search bar to open the filter panel.

The following filters are available:

| Filter | Description |
|---|---|
| **Date Range** | Filter by the record's upload date or original creation date. Set a start date, an end date, or both. |
| **Category** | Show only records assigned to a specific workspace category, such as "Contracts" or "Invoices". |
| **Tags** | Show only records that carry one or more of the tags you select. You can add multiple tags to the filter. |
| **File Type** | Narrow results to a specific media type: document, image, video, audio, spreadsheet, or archive. |
| **Uploaded By** | Filter by the team member who originally uploaded the record. Useful for reviewing a colleague's contributions or auditing your own uploads. |
| **Custom Fields** | Filter by the value of any custom metadata field your workspace admin has defined, such as "Expiry Date before 2025-01-01" or "Project Code equals REBRAND-04". |

When you apply multiple filters at once, they combine using **AND logic** — results must satisfy every condition simultaneously. To broaden your results, remove individual filter chips by clicking the **×** on each one, or click **Clear All** at the top of the filter panel.

<Note>
  Filters apply within the current context. If you are browsing a specific folder or collection when you open the filter panel, filters act on that folder or collection's contents only. To search the full archive, navigate to **All Records** in the sidebar first.
</Note>

## Sorting Results

Use the **Sort** dropdown in the top-right corner of the main record list to change the order of results. The available sort options are:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="By Date">
    - **Most Recent** — Records uploaded or created most recently appear first. This is the default sort order.
    - **Oldest First** — Useful when reviewing a historical archive in chronological order.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Alphabetical">
    - **Alphabetical (A → Z)** — Sort by record title in ascending alphabetical order.
    - **Alphabetical (Z → A)** — Sort by record title in descending alphabetical order.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="By Size">
    - **File Size** — Sorts from largest to smallest file. Helpful when auditing storage usage or identifying unexpectedly large uploads.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

Your sort preference persists within the current session. Switching folders or collections resets the sort to Most Recent.

## Saved Searches

If you run the same combination of keywords and filters regularly, save the search so you can re-run it in a single click.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Apply your filters and keyword">
    Set up your search query and any filter conditions exactly as you want them. Confirm that the results look correct.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the search">
    Click **Save Search** in the top-right of the results area, next to the Sort dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the saved search">
    Enter a descriptive name — for example, "Q3 Contracts expiring soon" or "Uploads by Jordan this month" — and click **Save**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access it from the sidebar">
    Your saved search appears in the sidebar under **Saved Searches**. Click it at any time to re-run the exact same query against the current state of your archive.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Workspace admins can share a saved search with the entire team. Open the saved search, click the **⋯** menu next to its name, and select **Share with Workspace**. The search then appears in every team member's sidebar under a **Shared Searches** section, making it easy to distribute standard audit queries or compliance checklists across your organization.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Combine a **Tags** filter with a **Date Range** filter to quickly audit records added in a specific period under a particular subject area. For example, filtering by the tag `vendor-invoice` and a date range of the last fiscal quarter gives you a precise, reproducible list for expense review — no manual scanning required.
</Tip>
