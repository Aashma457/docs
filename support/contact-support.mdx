The ORArchive support team is here to help you resolve issues, answer questions that go beyond what this documentation covers, and ensure your experience with the platform is smooth. Before reaching out, it is worth checking the [FAQ](/support/faq) and [Common Issues](/support/common-issues) pages — many questions are answered there immediately. If you need to speak with the team directly, use one of the channels below.

## Support Channels

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Email Support" icon="envelope">
    Send a message to **support@orachive.com** with your workspace name, a clear description of the issue, and any error messages you have seen. The team will reply within **1 business day** on Starter plans and above. Free plan users receive a response within 3 business days.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Live Chat" icon="comments">
    Click the **chat bubble** in the bottom-right corner of any ORArchive page to start a conversation with a support agent. Live chat is available **Monday–Friday, 9 AM–6 PM UTC** and delivers an immediate response during those hours. Outside business hours, messages are queued and answered the next working day.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Help Center" icon="book-open">
    Browse this documentation for answers to common questions. The [FAQ](/support/faq) covers account, billing, and file questions, while [Common Issues](/support/common-issues) provides step-by-step troubleshooting. Most routine questions are resolved here without needing to open a ticket.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Enterprise Support" icon="building">
    Enterprise customers are assigned a **dedicated account manager** and receive SLA-backed support with guaranteed response and resolution times. Contact your account manager directly using the contact details provided during onboarding. If you have misplaced those details, email support@orachive.com and reference your enterprise account.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What to Include in Your Support Request

To help the support team diagnose and resolve your issue as quickly as possible, include the following information in every request — whether you are writing an email or starting a live chat:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Workspace name or URL">
    Provide your workspace name or the full URL (for example, `acme-corp.orachive.com`). This allows the team to locate your account without delay.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Account email address">
    Include the email address you use to sign in to ORArchive. This confirms your identity and the plan you are on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="What you were trying to do">
    Describe the action you were attempting — for example, "I was uploading a PDF to the Legal > Contracts folder" — so the team understands the context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="What happened instead">
    Explain the unexpected behaviour, and include any **error messages verbatim** (copy and paste the exact text or attach a screenshot). Error codes and messages are often the fastest route to a diagnosis.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record ID or collection ID if applicable">
    If the issue relates to a specific record or collection, include its ID. You can find it in the URL bar when the record or collection is open — it appears as a string of characters after `/records/` or `/collections/`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browser and operating system">
    Include your browser name and version (for example, **Chrome 124**) and your operating system (for example, **Windows 11** or **macOS Sonoma**). Browser-specific bugs are common and this detail narrows down the cause quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Steps to reproduce the issue">
    List the exact steps that lead to the problem, numbered in order. If the team can reproduce the issue, they can fix it faster and verify the fix before closing your ticket.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Response Times

Response and resolution times vary by plan. The table below shows the targets you can expect after submitting a support request:

| Plan | First response | Resolution target |
|---|---|---|
| Free | 3 business days | Best effort |
| Starter | 1 business day | 5 business days |
| Professional | 4 business hours | 2 business days |
| Enterprise | 1 hour (SLA) | Custom SLA |

<Info>
  "Business hours" are defined as **Monday–Friday, 9 AM–6 PM UTC**, excluding public holidays. Requests submitted outside these hours are queued and responded to at the start of the next business day, except for Enterprise customers whose SLA covers 24/7 critical incidents.
</Info>

<Tip>
  Before submitting a ticket, check the **ORArchive Status Page** at [status.orachive.com](https://status.orachive.com) to see whether a known platform incident is already being investigated. If an outage or degraded performance is listed, your issue is likely being addressed at the infrastructure level and will resolve without a support ticket. Subscribe to status updates on that page to receive notifications when incidents are opened and resolved.
</Tip>
