ORArchive is designed to be straightforward, but you may still have questions as you get started or grow your usage. This page collects the most common questions from users across every plan — browse by topic or search for keywords to find the answer you need quickly.

## Account

<Accordion title="Can I use ORArchive for free?">
  Yes. The **Free plan** includes 10 GB of storage, up to 3 team members, and access to all core features — uploading, organising, searching, and sharing records. There are no time limits on the Free plan. When you are ready for more storage or additional team seats, go to **Settings > Billing** to upgrade at any time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I belong to multiple workspaces?">
  Yes. A single ORArchive account can belong to multiple workspaces — for example, one for your organisation and one for a personal project. Use the **workspace switcher** in the bottom-left corner of the sidebar to move between workspaces without signing in and out. Each workspace has its own storage quota, members, and billing.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I delete my account?">
  Go to **Settings > Account > Delete Account** and follow the confirmation steps. Deletion is permanent and removes all your records, files, and workspaces — there is no partial recovery once the process completes. However, you have a **30-day grace period** in which you can cancel the deletion by signing back in and clicking **Cancel Deletion**. After 30 days the deletion is irreversible.
</Accordion>

## Records & Files

<Accordion title="What file types does ORArchive support?">
  ORArchive accepts most common file formats, including:

  - **Documents:** PDF, DOCX, XLSX, PPTX, TXT, ODT
  - **Images:** PNG, JPG, JPEG, GIF, WEBP, TIFF, SVG
  - **Video & Audio:** MP4, MOV, AVI, MP3, WAV, AAC
  - **Archives:** ZIP, TAR, GZ

  For the complete and up-to-date list of supported formats, see the [Uploading Files](/records/uploading-files) guide. If you attempt to upload an unsupported format, ORArchive will display an error at the point of upload.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Is there a file size limit?">
  Yes. The maximum size for a single file upload depends on your plan:

  | Plan | File size limit |
  |---|---|
  | Free | 512 MB |
  | Starter | 2 GB |
  | Professional | 5 GB |
  | Enterprise | 5 GB (configurable) |

  If your file exceeds the limit for your plan, the upload will fail with a size error. Upgrade your plan or compress the file before uploading.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are my files backed up?">
  Yes. ORArchive stores every file with **redundant backups across multiple data centres**, protecting against hardware failure and data loss. Backups run continuously — you do not need to configure anything. **Enterprise plans** additionally include **geo-redundant storage**, which replicates data across geographically separate regions for higher resilience. You can review our data redundancy policy in the Trust & Security documentation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I recover a permanently deleted file?">
  No. Once a file is permanently deleted, it cannot be recovered — not even by the ORArchive support team. Files you delete are first moved to **Trash**, where they remain recoverable for up to **30 days**. You can restore them at any time during that window via **Trash > Restore**. After 30 days (or if you manually empty the Trash), the deletion becomes permanent.

  <Warning>
    Always double-check before emptying the Trash or confirming a permanent deletion. This action cannot be undone.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

## Sharing & Collaboration

<Accordion title="Can I share a record with someone who doesn't have an ORArchive account?">
  Yes. Generate a **shareable link** for the record by opening the record, clicking **Share**, and enabling the link under the **Link** tab. Recipients can view the record in their browser without creating an account. You can optionally set a password, an expiry date, or restrict access to view-only. For full details, see the [External Access](/sharing/external-access) guide.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I stop someone from accessing a shared record?">
  The method depends on how access was granted:

  - **Named user access:** Open the record → **Share** → click **×** next to the person's name to revoke their access immediately.
  - **Shareable link access:** Open the record → **Share** → **Link** tab → toggle the link off. Anyone with the old link will immediately lose access. You can also generate a new link, which invalidates the previous one.

  Changes take effect instantly — the person will see an "Access denied" message the next time they attempt to open the record.
</Accordion>

## Billing

<Accordion title="What payment methods does ORArchive accept?">
  ORArchive accepts the following payment methods:

  - **Credit and debit cards:** Visa, Mastercard, American Express
  - **PayPal**
  - **Invoice billing:** Available for Enterprise customers — contact your account manager to set up net-30 or net-60 invoicing.

  All card payments are processed securely through our payment provider and your card details are never stored on ORArchive servers.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I change my plan at any time?">
  Yes. You can upgrade or downgrade your plan at any time from **Settings > Billing**:

  - **Upgrades** take effect immediately. You are charged a prorated amount for the remainder of the current billing cycle, and your new storage and seat limits apply right away.
  - **Downgrades** take effect at the start of your next billing cycle. Your current plan features remain active until then.

  <Note>
    If you downgrade and your current storage usage exceeds the new plan's limit, uploads will be paused until you reduce your usage or upgrade again.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if I exceed my storage limit?">
  When your workspace reaches 100% of its storage quota, **new uploads are paused**. Existing files are unaffected and remain fully accessible. To resume uploading, you must either:

  1. **Upgrade your plan** to a higher storage tier via **Settings > Billing**, or
  2. **Free up space** by permanently deleting files you no longer need (remember to empty the Trash to reclaim the space).

  ORArchive sends you email warnings at 80% and 95% usage so you have time to act before uploads are blocked.
</Accordion>
