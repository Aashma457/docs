title: "ORArchive Quick Tour: From Upload to Sharing in Minutes"

---

The fastest way to understand how ORArchive works is to complete the full cycle yourself: get a file into the system, describe it so others can find it, put it in the right place, confirm you can retrieve it, and hand it off to a colleague. This tour guides you through each of those actions in sequence, using the same tools you'll reach for every day. By the end, you'll have a clear mental model of how records move through ORArchive and where everything lives.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload a File">
    Click the **Upload** button in the top bar — it's visible from every page in the interface. A file picker dialog opens. Select one or more files from your computer, or simply **drag and drop** files directly onto the main content area if you prefer.

    ORArchive accepts a wide range of formats, including **PDF, DOCX, XLSX, PNG, JPG, MP4**, and many others. Individual files up to **2 GB** are supported. If you're uploading multiple files at once, a progress indicator appears for each one, and you can continue navigating the interface while uploads run in the background.

    <Info>
      If an upload fails, ORArchive displays an error message next to the affected file with a brief explanation (for example, unsupported format or file size exceeded). Dismiss the error, resolve the issue, and try again.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Metadata">
    As soon as a file finishes uploading, the **record detail panel** slides open on the right side of the screen. This is your opportunity to describe the record before it gets buried in a growing archive.

    Fill in the following fields:

    - **Title** — Give the record a clear, descriptive name. Avoid generic titles like "Document1"; something like "Q3 2024 Vendor Contract – Acme Supplies" is far more useful.
    - **Description** — Add a sentence or two explaining what the record contains and why it exists. This text is fully indexed and appears in search results.
    - **Category** — Select the most appropriate category from the dropdown. Categories are defined at the workspace level and help with bulk filtering.
    - **Tags** — Type one or more tags and press **Enter** after each one. Tags are free-form keywords that make records easy to surface through search. Use tags for things that don't fit neatly into a category — for example, a project code, a client name, or a fiscal year.

    Click **Save** when you're done. The metadata is attached to the record immediately and is searchable right away.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Organize Into a Folder">
    With the record still open in the detail panel, click the **Move to Folder** option (found in the **Details** tab under the record's current location). A folder picker dialog appears, showing your existing folder structure.

    Select the folder where this record belongs. If the right folder doesn't exist yet, click **New Folder** within the dialog, type a name, and click **Create** — ORArchive creates the folder and moves the record into it in one step.

    Well-structured folders pay dividends as your archive grows. A consistent naming convention — such as `[Year] / [Department] / [Project]` — makes it easy for anyone on your team to know where to look without having to search every time.

    <Info>
      Records can only live in one folder at a time, but you can add a record to multiple **Collections** if you need it to appear in more than one context. Collections are covered in the organizing guides.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search for the Record">
    Click the **search bar** at the top of the sidebar — or press **/** on your keyboard — and type a keyword from the record's title or one of its tags. ORArchive searches across titles, descriptions, and tags in real time, updating results as you type.

    When your record appears in the results list, click it to open the detail panel and confirm everything looks correct. Notice that matching keywords are highlighted in the results, making it easy to see why a particular record was returned.

    To refine your results further, press **Enter** to open the full search page. There you'll find filter controls for file type, category, date range, tag, and owner — combine them to zero in on exactly the right records even in a large archive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share With a Colleague">
    Open the record you want to share and navigate to the **Sharing** tab in the detail panel. Click **Share**. An invitation dialog appears.

    Enter your colleague's **email address** in the input field. Then choose their **permission level**:

    - **View** — The recipient can open and download the record but cannot make any changes to it or its metadata.
    - **Edit** — The recipient can update the record's metadata, upload a new version of the file, and add comments.

    Click **Send Invite**. ORArchive sends your colleague an email notification with a direct link to the record. Once they accept, their name and permission level appear in the Sharing tab. You can change or revoke their access at any time from that same tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>
